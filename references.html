Sources & references

Every number on the India fiscal map is sourced from public datasets — Finance Commission reports, RBI's Handbook of Statistics on Indian States, MoSPI advance estimates, Union Budget receipts, and the CMS-India Corruption Study. Headline percentages (Finance Commission horizontal shares, FY24 budget actuals) are exact. State-level breakdowns (GSDP, own tax, devolution per year) are approximations within ±10%, smoothed for year-on-year comparability. Each section below documents what we used and what we are deliberately approximating.

01Finance Commission devolution

Vertical pool + horizontal shares (exact figures)
  • Report of the 14th Finance Commission (2015–2020) FC-XIV · Government of India · 2014
    Recommended raising the vertical share of central taxes devolved to states from 32% to 42%. Horizontal formula weights: population (17.5%), demographic change (10%), area (15%), forest cover (7.5%), income distance (50%). PDF
  • Report of the 15th Finance Commission (2021–2026) FC-XV · Government of India · 2020 (final)
    Lowered vertical share to 41% (absorbed J&K/Ladakh becoming UTs). New horizontal formula weights: population 2011 (15%), area (15%), forest & ecology (10%), income distance (45%), demographic performance (12.5%), tax effort (2.5%). PDF
  • Union Budget — Receipts & Devolution Ministry of Finance · annual
    Actual vertical devolution figures FY15–FY24 (used as the annual pool ranges in the dashboard's vertical_pool_actuals). indiabudget.gov.in

02State GSDP & own tax revenue

Yearly data, current prices (approximated within ±10%)
  • RBI — Handbook of Statistics on Indian States Reserve Bank of India · annual
    Authoritative state-wise dataset for own tax revenue, debt-GSDP, fiscal deficit. Used for own-tax and devolution receipts cross-checks. RBI handbook
  • MoSPI — State-wise GSDP (current prices) Ministry of Statistics & Programme Implementation · advance + provisional estimates
    Primary source for GSDP series FY15–FY24. Recent years use first/second advance estimates and will be revised. mospi.gov.in
  • CAG — State Finance Audit Reports Comptroller & Auditor General · annual, by state
    Used to sanity-check devolution-received and grants-in-aid figures for major states. cag.gov.in

03Contribution to Center (estimated)

Heavily approximated — methodology disclosure
  • CBDT / DGTS — Income & Corporate Tax Collections Direct taxes by region · annual
    Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Delhi, Gujarat dominate direct-tax collections — used as anchors for the relative scaling. incometaxindia.gov.in
  • GST Council — State-wise SGST + IGST monthly GST collections
    SGST is destination-based, but IGST at origin gives a partial proxy for production-side contribution. State-wise monthly figures published since 2017. gstcouncil.gov.in
  • PRS Legislative Research — State Finance Notes PRS India · per-state annual briefs
    Used for triangulation of central-share receipts and grants. prsindia.org

04Governance footprint

IAS cadre strength, state employees, corruption perception
  • DoPT — Civil List, IAS Cadre Strength Department of Personnel & Training · annual civil list
    Approved (sanctioned) cadre strength per state. Important: 25–40% of cadre officers are typically on Central deputation at any time — the dashboard number is structural cap, not on-ground headcount. dopt.gov.in
  • Pay Research Unit — State Govt Employee Census Ministry of Finance / state finance reports
    State govt direct employee counts (in lakh). Excludes contract, outsourced, and PSU workers — which can add 30–60% more. doe.gov.in
  • CMS-India — India Corruption Study 2019 Centre for Media Studies · last edition before COVID
    % of households reporting they paid a bribe to access a public service in the past 12 months — most rigorous state-level corruption survey publicly available. CMS resumed sporadic studies post-COVID but full state coverage hasn't been replicated. cmsindia.org
  • State govt department lists Each state's secretariat website
    Departments listed in the detail card are illustrative of each state's structure — the "back-office" vs "public-facing" classification is editorial, drawn from published CAG audit reports and budget allocations.

05United States

Sources for the USA dashboard (usa.html)
  • BEA — GDP by State (SAGDP series) Bureau of Economic Analysis · annual
    Current-dollar nominal GSP per state. Source of truth for all GSP / per-capita-GSP figures. bea.gov/data/gdp/gdp-state
  • Census — Annual Survey of State Government Finances US Census Bureau · annual
    State own-source tax revenue. census.gov/programs-surveys/state
  • Census Bureau Vintage Population Estimates US Census Bureau · annual
    Per-state population. Used for per-capita GSP. census.gov/topics/population/data
  • USAspending.gov + Pew Federal Funds tracker Treasury · federal grants to state governments
    Federal grants-in-aid (Medicaid, IIJA, education, etc.) flowing TO state govts. usaspending.gov
  • Rockefeller Institute — 'Balance of Payments' portal SUNY Rockefeller Institute · annual
    Estimates federal taxes paid by each state's residents + businesses. Methodologically harder than the transfers side because corporate-tax HQ ≠ activity origin. Treat figures as ±10% of point estimate. rockinst.org
  • PublicaMundi simplified state GeoJSON Open data · ~87 KB
    State polygon boundaries (50 states + DC + PR). github.com/PublicaMundi/MappingAPI

06Block / taluk / tehsil (sub-district)

V1 pilot: Kerala, Goa, Sikkim. Other states on roadmap.
  • Census of India 2011 — Sub-District Directory Office of the Registrar General & Census Commissioner
    Block / taluk / tehsil names + populations. Not available as a single open CSV — locked in per-state PDF tables on the official portal. V1 hand-encodes block names from these directories cross-checked with state revenue dept sites; populations deferred to V2. censusindia.gov.in
  • LGD (Local Government Directory) — Ministry of Panchayati Raj Official government source · POST-based, no open API
    Authoritative block / sub-district codes. Behind POST requests and stateful forms — requires per-state scraping with Selenium or undocumented endpoints. lgdirectory.gov.in
  • MGNREGA — block-level person-days & wage payments nrega.nic.in · monthly · not yet wired
    Largest centrally-sponsored scheme; reports at block level. No clean open API; data is hidden behind state→district→block dropdown forms. Wiring this gives a "rural employment delivery" view at block level. nrega.nic.in
  • Administrative head note structural fact, not data
    Each block is headed by a Tahsildar (revenue) and a Block Development Officer (development), both typically state civil service — not IAS. The IAS cadre stops at district HQ; only a tiny subset of younger IAS officers serve as Sub-Divisional Magistrates above the block, never AT the block.

Caveats & methodology

Per-state per-year figures are approximations within ±10% for GSDP, own tax, devolution, grants, and contribution. They are smoothed for year-on-year readability rather than reported to the exact rupee. Finance Commission horizontal shares are exact, sourced directly from the FC final reports.

Contribution to Center is necessarily an estimate. Most central tax incidence is destination-blind — GST is consumption-based, customs are collected at ports (not at origin of consumption), and corporate tax is collected at registered HQ (which concentrates in Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru regardless of where activity happens). The dashboard uses a GSDP-weighted, formal-sector-share-adjusted estimate.

Corruption perception is from a 2019 survey — pre-COVID, pre-DBT-acceleration. Treat as directional, not as a 2024 number.

"Back-office" vs "public-facing" department classification is editorial. It draws on published CAG audit findings (which department's spending shows up most often in irregularity reports) and budget composition (salaries vs program spend ratios), but ultimately reflects an opinionated read.
